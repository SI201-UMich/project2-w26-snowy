The current system for regulating short-term rentals is inadequate. This is because it lacks real-time repotings and fails to fully take into accound for unregistered properties.
Without real-time data sharing between the platform and the city, it makes it difficult to keep an eye on violations, allowing some to bypass the rules and reduce overall
accountability and fairness to the system. Something that can be improved is that the city could require platforms to provide real-time listing data and strengthen the enforcement 
mechanisms to make sure that all properties are properly registered. 

Something that is important when web scraping is if the site owner authorizes the access. Under CFAA, scraping a website without authorization is considered unauthorized access and leads to legal consequences. This is important because, even though the data is publicly accessible, this doesn't mean it doesn't violate the terms of service, and bypassing restrictions can still lead to creating legal risk. Because of this, developers has to consider if they have permission to web scrape even if the data is publicly accessible.
